Investment Case — Bull vs Bear Arguments
GPU monopoly — 92% AI market share
At $30K/GPU, no competitor has matched H100/H200/Blackwell performance. AMD is 2+ generations behind. CUDA ecosystem lock-in with 10M+ developers makes switching costs prohibitive.
Cisco 2000 risk — growth deceleration
Revenue grew 581% in FY24. That rate is mathematically impossible to sustain. If growth decelerates from 65% to 20%, the market multiple could re-rate violently — regardless of profits.
$500B AI capex 2026 — Goldman Sachs estimate
Global AI infrastructure spend keeps being underestimated. Every hyperscaler (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon) is racing to deploy Blackwell clusters. Demand consistently exceeds supply.
China export restrictions — $15B+ revenue risk
H20 chip ban already cost $15B+ in potential revenue. Escalation or broader export controls could block NVDA from China entirely — historically 20%+ of total revenue.
Blackwell Ultra + Rubin — multi-year runway
Q4 FY26 Blackwell revenue was record-breaking. Rubin architecture is in pipeline. Each new chip generation buys 2–3 more years of hyper-growth before competition can respond.
Custom ASICs threat from hyperscalers
Google TPUs, Amazon Trainium, Microsoft Maia, and Meta MTIA are all custom silicon projects reducing dependence on Nvidia GPUs. If ASICs take 30%+ of workloads, the TAM shrinks.
$51B net cash — fortress balance sheet
More cash than debt, $96.7B FCF, 0.07x D/E ratio. Nvidia can fund R&D, acquisitions, and $60B in buybacks without touching external capital. Cash is also the ultimate competitive weapon.
70%+ drawdowns are historically normal for NVDA
NVDA fell 82% in 2018-19, 68% in 2021-22, 43% in 2025. In your IRA, a 70% crash turns $200K into $60K. Will you hold, or will you panic-sell at the bottom?

Investment Verdict — MBA Banker's Assessment

Nvidia is the most financially exceptional company in the history of public markets by several metrics — 55.6% net margin, 101.5% ROE, 126% ROIC, 65% revenue growth, and $96.7B in free cash flow in a single year. Its PEG ratio of 0.55x makes it cheaper on a growth-adjusted basis than most S&P 500 stocks growing at 8%. The forward P/E of 20.8x for a company compounding earnings at 60%+ annually is the definition of undervalued.

The single honest risk: concentration. Your IRA would own one company in one sector. NVDA has crashed 82%, 68%, and 43% in the last 8 years — and recovered every time. If you can hold through the next inevitable 50–70% drawdown without selling, the 18-year math overwhelmingly favors multi-millionaire outcomes. If you cannot, pick VGT and sleep well. The stock is not the risk. Your own behavior during a crash is the risk.

Recommendation: Strong Buy for a high-risk, long-horizon IRA investor. Conviction level: 9/10.
